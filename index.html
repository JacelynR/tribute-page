Michael Jordan

The man synonymous with the game of Basketball

Take a brief glimpse of Michael Jordan's life:

  • 1963 - born in Brooklyn, New York
  • 1981 - MJ accepted a basketball scholarship to North Carolina
  • 1982 - He made the game-winning jump shot in the NCAA Championship game against Georgetown, led by future NBA rivial Patrick Ewing
  • 1984 - MJ left North Carolina University one year before he was to graduate and entered the NBA Draft. He was selected by the Chicago Bulls as the third overall pick.
  • 1987 - He became the only player other than Wilt Chamberlain to score 3,000 points in a season.
  • 1991 - MJ and the Chicago Bulls won their NBA Championship, after sweeping the Detroit Pistons in four-games.
  • 1993 - MJ and the Bulls won their third consecutive NBA Championship.
  • 1993 - Jordan announced his retirement, citing that he lost his desire to play the game.
  • 1994 - Jordan surprises the sports world by signing to a minor league baseball contract with the Chicago White Sox
  • 1995 - Jordan returns to the NBA with the Chicago Bulls in March
  • 1996 - Jordan founded a Chicago area Boys and Girls Club, and dedicated it to his father.
  • 1998 - MJ and the Bulls have their second three-peat, winning their sixth NBA Championship.
  • 1999 - Jordan retires for the second time.
  • 2001 - Jordan announces his return to the NBA to play for the Washington Wizards.
  • 2003 - On April 16th, Michael Jordan plays his final NBA game
